## Patterns & Judgment

### ICP
- Target: CEO, Executive Director, COO, Owner at mid-size behavioral health orgs (SUD + mental health, not MAT-only)
- Must accept commercial insurance — no insurance = no ICP
- Multi-location preferred but not required
- PE-backed orgs are good — under margin pressure, care about denial revenue
- Skip: MAT-only (different workflow), faith-based no-insurance, small independent family medicine

### Who to reach out to at each org
- Always try to get the CEO/Owner AND one operational person (COO, UR Director, ED)
- UR Director is the best champion — lives in the denial pain daily
- CFO is good at PE-backed orgs — cares about revenue leakage
- Program Directors are lower priority — usually not budget holders
- If no clear budget holder, go 2-3 deep and let replies tell you who cares

### Channel logic
- LinkedIn connect first if they're active on LinkedIn, then message once accepted
- Email works fine for most — behavioral health operators aren't LinkedIn-native
- Phone for older operators or people with no digital presence
- Text is fine for small owner-operators (e.g. James Marshall)
- Multi-touch: LinkedIn + email if you have both

### Message structure (what works)
- Open with something specific and real — a podcast they were on, a LinkedIn post, their dissertation, a connection to your background. No generic openers.
- State what you're building in one sentence — "AI system to fight insurance denials in behavioral health"
- The credibility line that works: VC-backed + pilots with early results + White House on overdose prevention policy + UChicago harm reduction
- Ask is always lunch or coffee, specific dates, low pressure
- "I grew up in Orlando / born and raised in Florida" does real work — makes it personal not sales
- "Would love to get your advice" is a strong close — positions them as expert not prospect
- "Can I bring you lunch" is easier yes than "grab lunch"
- Subject lines: short, specific, non-salesy. "Your LinkedIn post", "Lunch?", "Question?", "Free to meet?"

### Message voice (Eshan's)
- Direct, real, no corporate language
- "I think insurers are being negligent in their coverage" — takes a stance
- "I wanted to make something to fight back" — mission-driven framing
- Don't say "I believe" or "I think" before every point — just state it
- No AI-sounding words: robust, comprehensive, innovative, etc.
- Background paragraph works well at the END as a PS-style credential drop, not at the top
- Don't over-explain the product — one line is enough in the cold message

### What doesn't work
- Generic openers ("I came across your profile")
- Leading with the product before establishing a human connection
- "Every denial that sticks is a patient who loses care" — sounds performed
- Overlong messages — if it takes more than 30 seconds to read, cut it
- Asking for advice AND pitching in the same message — pick one

### Skip criteria
- No commercial insurance
- MAT-only (methadone clinics — different payer mix, mostly Medicaid)
- Single-location family medicine with addiction as a side service
- Faith-based / sliding scale / free programs
- Anyone where the role clearly isn't a budget holder AND there's no path to the budget holder
